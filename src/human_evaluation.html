Judging validity of answers to why questions in stories

Instructions

Thank you for participating in this HIT!

We are trying to gauge how humans and machines answer why-questions using information learned from a given story. 

You will be shown a short story, a related question and a possible answer to that question.

You will have to answer a few simple questions based on it.

 

  • Please read the story thoroughly.
  • An answer is stongly grammatical if it follows all the rules of English grammar.
  • It is grammatical if there is a mistake in tense, number, punctuation or something minor.
  • It is comprehensible if there are clear grammatical mistakes but its meaning can be inferred.
  • The validity of the answer is dependent on if the information presented in the answer can be inferred (implicitly or explicitly) from the story.
  • An answer is considered invalid if it does not give a plausible reason for the question asked and states an irrelevant piece of information instead.
  • The degree of relevance and validity of an answer can vary. Make sure you select the option you agree with most.

 

Some examples are provided below.
